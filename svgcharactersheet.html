Deck of Fortunes and Misfortunes

Sors Fortunae et Infortunae, Oracula Fortunae Dubiae, Orakle

This deck is a powerful tool for altering fate with every card drawn. Customize the deck with more than just standard cards for an unpredictable experience. Play with the cards as usual, or use the card rules to play a character whose fate is drawn from cards instead of dice.

The deck appears in the bottom-left corner.

  • Left-click the deck to draw a random card. It appears in a random place on the page.
  • The remaining card count is shown in the top-left of the deck face.
  • Right-click the deck (or use the shuffle button) to reshuffle all cards.
  • Right-click cards to discard, or drag them to the discard area.
  • Left-click a card to flip it over.
  • Mouse-over a face up card to get a tooltip with additional information.

Check yes under "Use images instead" to display illustrated tarot-style cards.

Add to the Deck

At the top of the Deck Builder to the Right under settings, use the dropdown to choose a preset deck: Standard Deck_52, Standard Joker Deck_54, Orakle Deck_82, Tarot Deck_78, etc.

Selecting custom enables you to build a deck from the options below. in custom mode, use the buttons in the Custom Card List to add individual cards.

The dropdown, custom card input, and checkboxes all contribute to the deck when you press apply. Search for the drawn card in the Lookup Engine for additional information.

  • Remove duplicate cards — Prevents repeating cards in the final deck.
  • Add a card back — Adds a mysterious card that some say can never be flipped.
  • 2 or 3 Jokers — Add chaos and variety with Jokers.
  • 4 Knights — Chivalric archetypes from the old country.
  • Major Arcana — 22 symbolic cards from the tarot.

Each card adds a unique effect, shaping destiny in novel ways.

Tooltip Format

When a card is flipped, the tooltip reveals key information at a glance:

  • Icon: The card’s symbolic emblem.
  • Name: The card’s standard name.
  • Orakle: A mystical description including its orakle name, cursed form, and illusion/motif.
  • Up or Down: Whether the card’s meaning is upright or reversed.
  • Motif/Illusion: What’s seen or what’s imagined — especially in illusion

Format: Icon & Card Name | Orakle Card | Cursed Version [Motif/Illusion] UP or DOWN meaning

Cursed Card Markers
  • * = Appears in the 13-card cursed deck
  • ** = Appears in the 22-card cursed deck
  • *** = Additional cursed cards beyond standard sets
  • **** = Game-ending consequences

Additional Custom Dice Inputs

You can use these input fields to store dice rolls linked to your character. For example, you might set Custom Slot 1 to roll damage for a special dagger that deals 2d4.

Use the format: [number of dice]d[sides] [+/- modifier]

Detailed Breakdown
  • Useful for custom attacks, spells, or item effects
  • 1dX or 1dx is used for the default die type (commonly a d20). 1dX cannot be chained.
  • You can include multiple dice and modifiers & additions and subtractions can be chained:
    Example: 2d6 + 1d4 - 1 Example: 2d4 - 3 + 4
  • Unusual syntax permitted:
    d20+2 → Missing number of dice; should be 1d20+2. 1d6++2 or 1d6--2 → Consecutive operators (takes the last operator as true). 1d6+ or 1d6- → Ignores trailing operators without a value as incomplete.
  • Examples of bad syntax:
    1dx+1dx → Default die (1dx) should appear only once. 3+1d4-2 → Must start with a dice roll, not a number. 1d4d4 → Invalid format; cannot nest dice like this.2dd6 → Extra "d"; correct format is 2d6. 2d+5 → Missing number of sides on the die. No syntax for exploding dice or other special mechanics.
